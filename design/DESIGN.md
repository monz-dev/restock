## Brand & Style

This design system is built on the principles of **Minimalism** and **Institutional Precision**. It is designed for high-density data environments where clarity and focus are paramount. The aesthetic is "Low-Light Professional," utilizing deep slate tones to reduce eye strain while maintaining a sophisticated, executive atmosphere.

The style avoids unnecessary ornamentation, relying instead on a strict mathematical grid, subtle tonal shifts, and a signature dotted background pattern that evokes technical blueprints. The emotional response is one of calm authority, reliability, and structured intelligence.

## Colors

Dark-mode 
The palette is anchored in **Deep Slate** tones to create a tiered dark-mode experience. 
- **Primary:** A muted sky blue (#38bdf8) used sparingly for high-priority actions and active states.
- **Surface Layering:** The base background uses #0f172a, while interactive cards and headers use #1e293b to create a sense of depth without relying on shadows.
- **Accents:** Muted slates and indigos are used for secondary information to ensure the hierarchy remains flat and unobtrusive.
- **Grid Pattern:** The background features a repeated dotted grid (1px dots, 24px spacing) in #1e293b to provide a subtle structural texture.

Light-mode
This design system utilizes a palette of serious, desaturated tones. The primary foundation is built on **Charcoal** and **Slate Gray**, providing a weighted, professional structure. The background uses an **Off-White** base to reduce eye strain during prolonged use.  Crucially, color is used functionally rather than decoratively. The four status indicators—Blue, Orange, Indigo, and Green—are the only vibrant elements permitted, ensuring they draw immediate attention to task states. Neon and violet hues are strictly excluded to maintain the "serious" tone of the dashboard.

## Typography

This design system utilizes a dual-font approach. **Manrope** is used for headlines to provide a modern, slightly rounded warmth that prevents the dashboard from feeling too cold or "terminal-like." **Inter** is utilized for all functional text, body copy, and data points due to its exceptional legibility at small sizes and its neutral, systematic character. 

For data-heavy tables, use the `data-mono` style which leverages Inter’s tabular num features to ensure columns of figures align perfectly.

## Layout & Spacing

The layout follows a **Fixed-Fluid Hybrid Grid**. The sidebar remains fixed at 280px, while the main content area utilizes a 12-column fluid system with wide 24px gutters. 

Spacing is strictly derived from a 4px baseline grid. Internal component padding should be generous (16px/12px) to maintain the "Minimalist" feel and avoid visual clutter. Group related data widgets with 24px gaps, while major functional sections should be separated by 48px to provide clear visual breathing room.

## Elevation & Depth

In dark-mode environment, depth is communicated through **Tonal Layering** rather than traditional shadows. 
- **Level 0 (Background):** #0f172a with the dotted grid pattern.
- **Level 1 (Cards/Panels):** #1e293b with a 1px solid border of #334155.
- **Level 2 (Dropdowns/Modals):** #1e293b with a more pronounced 1px border of #475569 and a very subtle, large-radius black shadow (0px 10px 30px rgba(0,0,0,0.5)).

Avoid "glow" effects or heavy blurs. All surfaces should feel solid and opaque.

In light-mode enviroment, depth is achieved through **low-contrast outlines** and **tonal layering** rather than traditional drop shadows. Surfaces are defined by 1px borders in a soft slate gray (#E2E8F0) to separate components from the background.

When a card or element requires focus (hover state), a very subtle, diffused ambient shadow may be used (e.g., 4px blur, 2% opacity charcoal). The primary method of showing hierarchy is "stacking"—where the background is the lowest level, cards are the second level, and modal overlays are the third. This ensures the UI remains flat and professional.

## Shapes

The shape language is "Soft-Technical." A consistent 4px (0.25rem) radius is applied to standard components like input fields, buttons, and chips. Larger containers and cards use an 8px (0.5rem) radius. 

This subtle rounding balances the "Institutional" rigidity of the grid with a modern, professional approachability. Use sharp 0px corners only for data-table headers or dividers that span the full width of a container.

## Components dark-mode

- **Buttons:** Primary buttons use a solid #38bdf8 background with dark text (#0f172a). Secondary buttons use a ghost style: #1e293b background with a #334155 border and #94a3b8 text.
- **Input Fields:** Use #0f172a for the field background to create an "inset" look against the #1e293b cards. Borders should brighten to #38bdf8 only on focus.
- **Chips:** Small, low-contrast pills using #334155 background and #94a3b8 text. No borders.
- **Data Tables:** Row separators use 1px solid #334155. Hover states for rows should use a subtle tint of #1e293b at 50% opacity.
- **Cards:** Standard dashboard widgets should have a clear title in `label-caps` typography, separated by a thin horizontal rule from the content.
- **Dotted Grid:** Apply the `grid_dot` variable as a CSS background-image radial gradient to the main layout wrapper to maintain the requested technical aesthetic.

## Components light-mode

### Cards
Cards are the primary container for information. They feature a white background, a 1px soft slate border, and no shadow. The header of the card should be separated by a subtle horizontal rule or a slight tonal shift in background color.

### Status Indicators (Chips)
Status chips are critical for navigation. They consist of a light tint background of the status color with a darker text/icon of the same hue:

- **Pendiente:** Light Orange background, Orange text.
- **Despachado:** Light Indigo background, Indigo text.
- **Entregado:** Light Green background, Green text.

### Buttons
Action buttons are simple and flat. The primary action button uses the Charcoal color with white text. Secondary buttons use a white background with a Slate Gray border. Interactions (hover/active) should only result in a subtle tonal shift (darker or lighter) of the base color.

### Input Fields
Inputs are minimalist, using the same 1px border as cards. When focused, the border color shifts to the primary Charcoal or Blue, without adding any outer glow or shadow.

### Background Grid
The background is not a solid color but a "canvas" featuring a 1px dotted pattern in `#E2E8F0`. This provides a subtle texture that helps the user's eye align elements across the horizontal and vertical axes of the dashboard.
